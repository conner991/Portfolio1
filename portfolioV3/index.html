Who I am

Born and raised in rural Oregon, I moved to Reno, Nevada in 2012 to branch out and see what else life had to offer. Getting out of the woods and into a city turned out to be the best decision I ever made. While extremely challenging, the move pushed me to start developing myself as an adult by establishing better social skills and becoming more in tune with my morals and interests. Exploring new places and meeting new people allowed me to start figuring out what kind of person I wanted to be and as time went on, my life took on more meaning and purpose because of it. This is why I believe doing difficult things will not only teach us powerful lessons about who we are, but will also shape us for the better in ways we can't even imagine.

I've always had an interest in the human experience and science. It started with astronomy and expanded into the realms of philosophy, psychology, sociology, political science, and eventually computer science. I find all aspects of human culture and society fascinating but as of the last few years, the aspects that involve our relationship with technology have captivated me the most. I believe that technology is an incredible tool that we have at our disposal. Using it not only makes simple things in our lives easier -- things that people tend to take for granted -- but it also changes the way we live and interact with the world in a deeply profound way. For the first time in humain history, we now have the ability to collectively choose to either help make the world a better place, or do nothing and make it worse. We are living in an amazing time for technological development in human society and that gives me hope and drive; hope for the possibility of a better tomorrow through inovation and knowledge building, and drive to be a part of that.
